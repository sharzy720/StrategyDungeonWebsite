Mid-2012 MacBook Pro

By: Johnathyn Strong
3-23-2022

I have found that a mid-2012 MacBook Pro is the oldest I would go when looking for a cheap laptop. These laptops can usually be found for $100 to $300 on eBay. This is a great price for what you get compared to similarly priced windows laptops. These laptops have an aluminum unibody chassis and decent performance.

Design

13-inch ports
15-inch ports

The design of the 2012 MacBook Pros still feel modern even though the design is almost 20 years old at this point. For ports these laptops come with a plethora compared to the MacBookâ€™s of today. On the left side of the laptop, you have a first gen MagSafe port, Ethernet, FireWire, one thunderbolt 2, two usb, sd card reader, headphone jack, and a battery indicator. While on the right side you only have a dvd/cd drive and a Kensington lock. The only difference between the 13-inch and 15-inch port wise is that the 15-inch has separate line in and line out ports while the 13-inch has a combo jack. Since these MacBooks are thicker then newer models they can be little heavier. The 15-inch weighs in at 5.6 pounds while the 13-inch weighs in at 4.5 pounds.

Performance

The 2012 MacBook Pros still pull their weight when compared to modern budget laptops. However, it does need to be kept in mind that these laptops are running hardware from 10 years ago. There are two main versions of the 2012 MacBook pro, the 13-inch and the 15-inch. Both models share their physical design aside from the screens and the internal hardware.

13-inch models

CPU RAM GPU
2.5GHz I5-3210M 4gb Integrated
2.9GHz I7-3520M 8gb Integrated

The 13-inch model was released in 2 options a 2.5GHz i5 with 4gbs of ram and a 2.9GHz i7 with 8gbs of ram. Both configurations are good enough for lightweight modern use like web browsing, word processing, and emails. The only downside of the i5 configuration is the 4gbs of ram which is quite low compared to modern computers. Luckily these laptops can be upgraded after purchase, meaning you can get a model with the 4gbs of ram and upgrade it to 8gbs or even 16gbs. The other big performance impact will be if the purchased model has a hard drive or SSD. If the purchased model is running a hard drive, then it will much slower than one running a SSD. However, just like with the ram the hard drive can be upgraded to an SSD after the fact. The only part that cannot be upgraded after the fact that makes this laptop show its age is the screen, which has a resolution of 1280 by 800. The low resolution of the screen can make it feel slightly cramped. However, for the price the quality of the screen will beat out other similar modern laptops.

15-inch models

CPU RAM GPU
2.3GHz I7-3615QM 4gb NVIDIA GeForce GT 650M 512mb
2.6GHz I7-3720QM 8gb NVIDIA GeForce GT 650M 1gb
2.7GHz I7-3820QM 8gb NVIDIA GeForce GT 650M 1gb

The 15-inch model was released in 3 options a 2.3Ghz i7 with 4gbs of ram and a 2.6Ghz or 2.7Ghz i7 with 8gbs of ram. All the models were released with a dedicated GPU, the NVIDIA GeForce GT 650M with either 512MBs or 1Gb of vram. Most of the points made about the 13-inch model still apply to the 15-inch model like, upgrading the ram and storage. When talking about performance the CPUs in the 15-inch can be roughly double the power of the ones in the 13-inch. As well as having better CPU performance the dedicated GPU in the 15-inch will shred the GPU in the 13-inch. The 15-inch also has a higher resolution screen which makes it feel more modern than the 13-inch. This higher resolution display came in 3 options a 1440 by 900 glossy display and a 1680 by 1050 glossy or anti-glare display. All the differences from the 13-inch makes this laptop able to do much more and handle heavier workloads like, light photo/video editing and gaming.

Purchasing suggestions

For what models I would recommend purchasing. The 13-inch with a 2.9GHz i7 with 8GBs of ram is what I would shoot for since the i7 will allow for more headroom when multitasking and completing tasks. While with the 15-inch I would recommend looking for a model that has the higher resolution display, as for the specs the 2.6GHz or 2.7Ghz i7 with 8Gbs of ram would be my go-to since those two options are the only way to get the dedicated GPU with 1GB of vram. As for pricing I would not spend more than $300 on one of these laptops, even $300 is pushing it these days since you can get a newer 2015 MacBook pro for that price.
